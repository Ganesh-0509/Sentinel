# On-site emergency response — gas release / fire

## Declaration and first actions

On confirmation of an uncontrolled flammable or toxic gas release, the shift in-charge
declares an on-site emergency. The immediate priorities, in order:

1. **Stop the source** where it can be done without exposing anyone — remote isolation,
   emergency shutdown of the affected section.
2. **Remove ignition sources.** Suspend all hot work in the affected area and any adjoining
   area that could be reached by the gas cloud. Do not operate electrical isolators in a
   suspected flammable atmosphere.
3. **Evacuate** personnel from the affected zone by the designated routes to the assembly
   point, moving crosswind or upwind of the release.
4. **Account for personnel** at the assembly point against the permit and roster records.
   Any unaccounted person is treated as a casualty until confirmed otherwise.
5. **Alert** the emergency response team, the control room, and, where the release may
   travel beyond the boundary, the external authorities.

## Zones

The affected area is treated in three zones: the **hot zone** (immediate release area, entry
only by trained responders with breathing apparatus), the **warm zone** (buffer and
decontamination), and the **cold zone** (assembly and command). Zone boundaries move with
wind direction and gas readings and must be re-assessed continuously.

## Communication

A single incident commander directs the response. Communication uses the designated
channel; radios that are not intrinsically safe must not be used in the hot zone. Status
is reported at fixed intervals to avoid channel congestion.

## Evidence preservation

Once life-safety actions are underway, the state of the plant at the time of the incident
must be preserved: process trends, gas readings, alarm and interlock history, the status of
all active permits, and the equipment's isolation state. This record is required for the
statutory investigation, and it degrades quickly if not captured deliberately — historians
roll over, and alarm buffers overwrite.

## Reporting

A preliminary notification goes to the prescribed authority within the required timeframe,
followed by a full investigation report. The report should establish the sequence of
events, the immediate and underlying causes, whether the hazard was detectable in advance,
and the corrective actions with owners and target dates.

## Return to normal

Re-entry and restart are authorised only after gas testing confirms a safe atmosphere, the
source of release is confirmed isolated, damaged equipment is assessed, and the incident
commander formally stands the emergency down.
